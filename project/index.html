About London

London, the capital of England, is a city of rich history, diverse culture, and iconic landmarks. From the Tower of London to the bustling markets, there's something for everyone.

Tower Bridge

One of London's most famous landmarks, offering stunning views over the River Thames.

Completed in 1894, Tower Bridge is a marvel of Victorian engineering. The bridge was designed to allow large ships to pass through, with its iconic twin towers connected by high walkways. Visitors can explore the bridge's historic engine rooms, learn about its construction, and take in panoramic views from the glass floor walkways. A symbol of London's architectural prowess, Tower Bridge is a must-see for any visitor.

Tower of London

A historic castle and home to the Crown Jewels, with centuries of fascinating history.

The Tower of London, built by William the Conqueror in 1078, is one of the most famous fortified buildings in the world. This UNESCO World Heritage site has served as a royal palace, prison, treasury, and even a zoo. Today, it's home to the Crown Jewels, a collection of regalia used in British coronations, including the Imperial State Crown. Visitors can also learn about the tower's storied history with tales of famous prisoners, ghost stories, and the legendary ravens who are said to protect the kingdom.

Big Ben

The iconic clock tower at the Palace of Westminster, a symbol of London.

Big Ben, officially known as the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster, is one of London's most recognizable landmarks. The clock tower, now called the Elizabeth Tower, was completed in 1859. It stands 96 meters tall and is famous for its accurate timekeeping and massive bell. Each New Year's Eve, Big Ben's chimes are broadcast worldwide, symbolizing the start of a new year. Tourists and locals alike admire the tower for its Gothic design and historical significance.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your trip with our expert travel tips for navigating London.

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and fall offer the best weather with fewer crowds.
  • Local Etiquette: Stand on the right on escalators in the Tube, and avoid loud conversations in public transport.
  • Transportation: Get an Oyster card for convenient travel across the city.
